The dominant players in blood coagulation monitoring today — all relying on invasive, intermittent blood testing. None offer continuous or non-invasive monitoring.

Point-of-care coagulation analyzer used in clinics and hospital outpatient settings. Handheld, single-use strips. Faster than lab testing but still requires a blood draw and provides only a single snapshot result.

Blood draw required · Snapshot only · No home use

Leading laboratory coagulation analyzer used by hospital labs worldwide for high-volume PT, aPTT, fibrinogen, and anti-Xa testing. Stago commands a major share of the clinical lab coagulation market globally.

Lab-only · Hours turnaround · No bedside or home use

Key Customer & Decision-Maker Segments

Cardiac Anesthesiologists Key decision-maker

The primary coagulation decision-makers in the OR — not the surgeon. Control heparin dosing and anticoagulation protocols. High influence on device adoption and hospital purchasing decisions.

Cardiac Surgeons

Primary end-user in the cardiac surgery beachhead. Benefit from real-time coagulation feedback during CPB and ECMO. Clinical champions for hospital purchasing decisions.

Perfusionists

Operate the CPB machine during cardiac surgery. Direct users of the KADI probe in the OR. Process-oriented — value workflow integration and ease of use.

Intensivists / ICU Physicians

Manage post-surgical patients on ECMO and anticoagulation therapy. High demand for continuous data; currently rely on intermittent blood draws every 1–2 hours.

DOAC Patients (Home)

~6M Americans on direct oral anticoagulants (rivaroxaban, apixaban, dabigatran). No home monitoring option exists today. RPM reimbursement via CPT codes 99454/99457 is a viable revenue stream.

Hematologists

Manage complex coagulopathies, bleeding disorders, and anticoagulation regimens. Potential clinical champions for hospital-wide adoption and research partnerships.

Business Model Overview

Clinical / Hospital Revenue

Durable hardware sale with no consumables. One-time device placement plus service contract. No per-test reagent costs — a key differentiator from TEG/ROTEM which require costly cartridges.

Home Monitoring Revenue

Device sale plus remote patient monitoring (RPM) reimbursement. CPT codes 99454 and 99457 enable physician billing for continuous monitoring data. Subscription model also feasible.

Data & Software (Future)

Future layer: coagulation trend analytics, AI-assisted dosing recommendations, and EHR integration. Revenue from SaaS / data services alongside hardware.

Regulatory Pathway

Clinical device likely requires 510(k) clearance. Home device pathway to be determined with a regulatory advisor. FDA Breakthrough Device Designation may apply given unmet need.
