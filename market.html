Current Monitoring Methods & Their Limitations

Method Setting Turnaround Blood Draw? Continuous? Key Limitation
ACT (Activated Clotting Time) OR, cath lab, ECMO 2–5 min Yes No Intermittent only; creates monitoring blind spots during surgery
TEG (Thromboelastography) Hospital lab, major ORs 10–30 min Yes No Slow turnaround; batch testing; expensive equipment
ROTEM (Rotational Thromboelastometry) Hospital lab, major ORs 10–30 min Yes No Same as TEG; operator-dependent; not real-time
PT / INR Lab (send-out) Hours to days Yes No No continuous monitoring; lab send-out delays therapy adjustment
aPTT Hospital lab 1–2 hours Yes No Lab-based; batch; not suitable for bedside or home
Anti-Xa Assay Hospital lab 1–2 hours Yes No DOAC monitoring only; lab-based; not point-of-care
CoaguChek (Roche) Home / clinic 1–2 min Yes — finger prick No Warfarin only; cannot monitor DOACs; requires daily finger stick

Key Customer & Decision-Maker Segments

Cardiac Anesthesiologists Key decision-maker

Identified in Week 3 I-Corps as the primary coagulation decision-makers in the OR — not the surgeon. Control heparin dosing and anticoagulation protocols. High influence on device adoption.

Cardiac Surgeons

Primary end-user in cardiac surgery beachhead. Benefit from real-time coagulation feedback during CPB and ECMO. Champions for hospital purchasing decisions.

Perfusionists

Operate the CPB machine. Direct users of KADI probe in the OR clinical setting. Very process-oriented — value workflow integration and ease of use.

Intensivists / ICU Physicians

Manage post-surgical patients on ECMO and anticoagulation therapy. High demand for continuous data; currently rely on intermittent blood draws.

DOAC Patients (Home)

~6M Americans on direct oral anticoagulants (rivaroxaban, apixaban, dabigatran). No home monitoring option exists today. RPM reimbursement via CPT 99454/99457 highlighted as revenue stream.

Hematologists

Manage complex coagulopathies, bleeding disorders, and anticoagulation regimens. Potential clinical champions for hospital-wide adoption.

Business Model Overview

Clinical / Hospital Revenue

Durable hardware sale (no consumables model). One-time device placement with service/maintenance contract. No per-test reagent costs — key differentiation from TEG/ROTEM.

Home Monitoring Revenue

Device sale + remote patient monitoring (RPM) reimbursement. CPT codes 99454 and 99457 enable physician billing for continuous monitoring data. Subscription model possible.

Data & Software (Future)

Future layer: coagulation trend analytics, AI-assisted dosing recommendations, integration with hospital EHR systems. Revenue from SaaS / data services.

Regulatory Pathway

FDA regulatory strategy to be defined. Clinical use device likely requires 510(k) clearance. Home device pathway to be determined in consultation with regulatory advisor.
